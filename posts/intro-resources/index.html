Resources for Prospective Politics Students

If you are thinking about studying Politics or want to know more about what the subject might look like, the following material can provide you an accessible introduction, in different media, to some themes and approaches in the social and political sciences:

Books

  • Daron Acemoglu, and James A. Robinson. Why Nations Fail: The Origins of Power, Prosperity, and Poverty. (Profile Books, 2013)
  • Francis Fukuyama. The Origins of Political Order: From Prehuman Times to the French Revolution. (Profile Books, 2012)
  • Francis Fukuyama. Political Order and Political Decay: From the Industrial Revolution to the Globalization of Democracy. (Profile Books, 2015)
  • Jon Elster. Nuts and Bolts for the Social Sciences. (Cambridge University Press, 1989)

Very Short Introductions

  • Bernard Crick. Democracy: A Very Short Introduction. (Oxford University Press, 2002)
  • David Miller. Political Philosophy: A Very Short Introduction. (Oxford University Press, 2003)
  • Kenneth R. Minogue. Politics: A Very Short Introduction. (Oxford University Press, 2000)
  • Cas Mudde, and Cristóbal Rovira Kaltwasser. Populism: A Very Short Introduction. (Oxford University Press, 2017)
